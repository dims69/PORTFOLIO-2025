A dark mode website for a dental prosthetics lab, with a built-in tool that lets dentists upload their 3D files straight to the lab's server.

Client Laboratoire 32
Year 2025
Role Design & Dev
Type Website & Business Tool

The Context

Laboratoire 32, based near Lyon, manufactures dental prosthetics in France. They combine traditional craftsmanship with digital tools (CAD/CAM). They had zero online presence and needed a website that matched the quality of their work.

The Challenge

Two problems to solve. First, build a visual identity from their existing logo. Then, find a way for dentists to send their 3D scan files without relying on emails or USB drives.

Goals

  • Build a "Dark & Gold" brand system from the logo colors
  • Add visual effects (WebGL particles via Vanta.js)
  • Automate 3D digital impression intake
  • Connect the site to the lab's local server (NAS)

My approach

I went with a dark mode and gold accents. The particle animation in the background (Vanta.js) gives it a technical feel without cluttering the page. For the business side, I built a "Practitioner Portal": the dentist uploads a file on the website, and it lands directly on the lab's NAS. No more emails, no more USB sticks.

Results

Brand Image

The "Petrol Blue & Gold" palette stands out from typical white medical websites. The lab makes a strong visual impression from the first visit.

WebGL Effects

Vanta.js particles in the background and "Stacking Cards" on scroll give the site a technical edge that fits the client's industry. It stays lightweight and readable.

Productivity

The workflow is fully automated: Form, API, OneDrive, then NAS. 3D files reach the lab's production server with zero manual handling.
