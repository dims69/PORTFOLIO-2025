Apidia

WordPress Elementor UI/UX Design Redesign

Website redesign and logo refresh for a plastics engineering company near Lyon. I pulled the colors and visual tone directly from the updated logo, with 3D backgrounds to match the technical nature of the business.

Client Apidia
Location Vaugneray (69)
Role Design & Integration
Type B2B/B2C Website

The Context

Apidia does machining, bending and bonding of plastics, for businesses and individuals. Their website had aged: outdated design, not mobile-friendly, and no clear showcase of their services. It all needed to be redone.

The Challenge

Start from the logo to rebuild the entire image. Refresh it first, then build a responsive site that highlights their services and makes it easy to request a quote.

Key steps

  • Logo refresh (updated typefaces and colors)
  • Derive the site's color scheme from the logo
  • Full mockups in Figma
  • WordPress build with Elementor

My approach

I used 3D visuals and wireframe drawings as page backgrounds, similar to what you'd see in CAD software like SolidWorks. It ties directly into their business. The colors come from the refreshed logo: a technical teal-green, with a clean layout to keep things readable and professional.

View live site ↗

Results

Modernized Image

The logo was refreshed without losing its DNA: new typefaces, new colors, but the core identity stays. The site follows the same logic. Modern without erasing the past.

Clear Navigation

Each service (machining, bending, bonding) has its own page. The contact form is streamlined so quote requests come in more easily.

Technical Atmosphere

3D visuals and wireframe backgrounds echo the CAD tools of the trade. Visitors immediately understand they're on a plastics engineering specialist's site.
