Branding Brand Strategy Art Direction

Brand creation for Groupe Loisirs Solutions. The group owns three golf courses and wanted to bring them under one name, with an identity that speaks to golfers, families, and food lovers alike.

Client Groupe Loisirs Solutions
Role Art Direction
Tools Illustrator, Photoshop
Deliverables Identity, Logo, Materials

The Context

Groupe Loisirs Solutions runs three golf courses in the same area. Each one had its own branding, its own materials, with no connection between them. The idea: create a shared brand to pool marketing efforts while keeping what makes each location unique.

The Challenge

Two things to figure out. First, give a shared identity to three golf courses that were each doing their own thing. Then, break away from golf's exclusive image to also attract families and beginners.

Brand Pillars

  • Accessibility: A laid-back golf experience, open to everyone.
  • Services: Highlight the setting and the dining options.
  • Consistency: One brand across 3 different locations.
  • Warmth: Welcome non-golfers too.

My Approach

I designed a leaf-shaped logo that forms an "E", with a small dot at the top to recall a golf ball. The gradient goes from deep green to light green to evoke the natural landscape of the courses. The typeface has a deliberately upscale feel, which positions the brand as premium without being over the top. The whole thing stays clean and welcoming, even for someone who's never picked up a club.

Results

Unified Communication

All three courses now share the same materials, the same tone, the same visual world. Each keeps its own character, but the group speaks with one voice.

Broader Audience

The "nature and wellness" positioning opens doors beyond golfers. Families, food lovers, curious visitors: golf becomes a destination, not just a sport.

Consistent Image

From the website to course signage, apparel and merchandise, everything is built from the same guidelines. The group gains credibility.
